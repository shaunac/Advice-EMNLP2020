<center>Advice Annotation</center>

Instructions

As we navigate through life, we encounter many challenges and questions (e.g., about education, career, relationships, parenting, life decisions, etc). Online forums have become good places for us to look for advice. However, navigating through many posts is a daunting task, and our goal is to help people find the advice that they actually need. And we need your help!

In this hit, we ask you to:

  1. read a post asking for advice,
  2. go to the link provided below and head to our highlighting interface to read the replies to this post,
  3. as you read through the replies, highlight text spans that give advice. This could be part of a sentence, a single sentence, or multiple sentences.

Specifically, an advice is text that the answerer intends the original poster to perceive as a directive to do something that addresses the poster’s question (to their benefit). Mark the longest contiguous text for each piece of advice. It may be helpful to read the entire text once and only after that mark the text snippets describing advice.

Advice can come in a variety of forms, for example:

  • Direct advice:
    • Using words such as "suggest", "advise", “recommend”
    • Using verbs such as “can”, “could”, “should”, “may”, “might”, “need”, e.g., "you should...", "you need to..."
    • Using imperatives, such as "Put baking soda and vinegar down the drain", or "Don’t get worked up because of the tantrum"
    • Using questions, such as "why don’t you…s", "how about…", "have you thought about…"
    • Expressing conditions, for example, "If I were you, I would…"
  • Indirect advice:
    • Sentences like "One thing you could do is…", "It won’t hurt to…"
    • Hints from personal experiences with the intention for the original poster to do the same thing, for example,
      • Q: I am having heart palpitations. What should I do?
      • A: “I also had palpitations. I stumbled upon fish oil that my dad was taking for his heart, and it cleared them out.”
    • Statements that imply an action should (or should not) be taken; below, the answer is clearly directing the original poster not to talk about an issue:
      • Q: Do you parents have any advice on how I should handle this going forward?
      • A: “Talking about it likely was making them more stressed out”

A good rule of thumb is to see whether the text instructs the original poster to do something that would solve the question. Here are some examples that should NOT be considered an advice:

  • Expressing sentiment, e.g., “Parenthood: you will never love and hate something so much.”, “It sounds to me that his family is uneducated in health”.
  • Expressing support, e.g., "You are enough.", "It’s totally understandable."
  • Generic argumentation, e.g., "Everyone is responsible for their own deeds."
  • Information-seeking questions, e.g., "How old is your daughter?"

The highlighting interface looks like below:

Each reply is marked by a reply ID; sub-replies are also marked with the symbols “>>>>”.

Figure 1: Markup dialog window. A "delete" button will appear for a span already marked.

To mark a portion of the text, highlight the text. A menu will appear. At the top, in the "Text" field you will see the selected text. The "Entity type" section has the "Advice" tag selected by default. Select "OK" at the bottom right to finalize the mark-up.

If you wish to delete a mark-up section, double click on the existing label, then select "Delete" at the bottom right. If you would like to edit an existing highlight, please first delete the original highlight.
